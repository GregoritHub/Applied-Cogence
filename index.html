Seven‑Year Arc Overview

The Applied Cogency program unfolds across seven years. Each year cycles through the four quadrants — I (Authentic Resonance), We (Moral Truth), It (Aesthetic Truth), and Its (Semantic Truth). After the first cycle the quadrants return at increasingly sophisticated resolutions. All deliverables, metrics and failure controls are hard‑wired to prevent drift.

Year 1 – Foundation

Breadth and scaffolding: Authentic Resonance, Moral Truth, Aesthetic Truth, Semantic Truth.

Year 2 – Operation

Function dynamics, virtue habituation, feedback.

Year 3 – Mechanism

Models, techniques and repeatable craft.

Year 4 – Expression

First full stack of practice in each quadrant.

Year 5 – Consolidation

Deepening with advanced texts and paid/library sources.

Year 6 – Synthesis

Cross‑quadrant problems pairing authority with symbol and form with justice.

Year 7 – Transmission

Teaching, codifying and reducing the practice to methods.

This site serves as your single hub for what to study, how to study it, when to do it, why it matters, and how to measure your progress along the way.

Yearly & Quarterly Breakdowns

Each year is broken into four quarters (except where noted). The details below are mirrored in the calendar view.

Year 1 — Foundation

  • Q1 Authentic Resonance: SEP/IEP entries; Jung Platform; MDR; dream journal daily; 12 typed summaries; 4 active‑imagination transcripts. Why: establish personal resonance and self‑awareness.
  • Q2 Moral Truth: John; Romans 1–12; Athanasius; Augustine VIII & XIX; Aquinas I (selected); 12 moral case briefs; service hours ≥20. Why: ground moral understanding in classical texts.
  • Q3 Aesthetic Truth: Plato Symposium/Philebus; Plotinus I.6; Aquinas ST I Q5/Q39; Kant §§1–22; 12 object sketches; 4 museum studies. Why: explore beauty and form.
  • Q4 Semantic Truth: Bertalanffy excerpts; Wiener ch.1–3; Bateson essays; Foerster; 2 small data models; 1 interface wireframe. Why: introduce systems thinking.

Year 2 — Operation

  • Q1: Jung’s Psychological Types I–XI; 8‑function map (own + 3 interlocutors); weekly dream‑type correlation; Beebe/von Franz selections. Why: operationalize psychological functions.
  • Q2: Aristotle Nicomachean Ethics I–II, III–V, X; Aquinas I–II Q55–70; habit protocol (2 virtues, micro‑drills, weekly examen); 8 dilemma write‑ups. Why: habituate virtues.
  • Q3: Kant §§30–60; Ruskin; SEP “Aesthetic Judgment”; 4 formal critiques; typographic drills (spacing, grids); 1 poster system. Why: refine aesthetic judgment.
  • Q4: Wiener ch.4–8; Ashby Introduction to Cybernetics; Shannon paper; Beer lectures; build 1 feedback loop in code; run 2 usability tests. Why: implement cybernetic feedback.

Year 3 — Mechanism

  • Q1: Socionics (Augusta → Gulenko); Model A; intertype relations; type‑interview 10 people; write 20‑page synthesis. Why: mechanize typological models.
  • Q2: Applied ethics frameworks (Markkula → SEP domain entries); Sandel lectures; 6 case memos (bio/war/tech/env/business/privacy). Why: apply ethical techniques.
  • Q3: Typography/Design (Müller‑Brockmann → Tschichold → Lupton); grid library; type scale; publish 12‑page booklet. Why: craft design mechanisms.
  • Q4: UX/IA (Garrett abridged → NNGroup guides; Krug; Norman revised); 1 research study (n ≥ 8); card‑sort; IA tree; interactive prototype. Why: build UX mechanisms.

Year 4 — Expression

  • I – Typological Practice: offer 10 pro‑bono sessions; keep consented notes; record outcome measures; counter‑transference log. Why: express typology in practice.
  • We – Governance Survey: Plato → Federalist → UDHR; write 3 policy briefs (1 200 words each) with virtue‑analysis rubric; run 2 dialogue circles. Why: express moral governance.
  • It – Designed Artifact: build a small type system (PDF + web); gather open critique; release a version 2 with revisions; document rationale. Why: express aesthetic design.
  • Its – Interface/Comms System: release a v1 productised tool (docs + onboarding); collect telemetry; iterate twice. Why: express semantic systems.

Year 5 — Consolidation

  • I: CW9i Archetypes; sample Mysterium Coniunctionis; von Franz or Hillman; conduct 2 symbol studies with amplification. Why: deepen archetypal understanding.
  • We: Aquinas I–II Q90–97; O’Donovan Resurrection & Moral Order or Ratzinger Introduction to Christianity; write 1 moral ontology essay. Why: consolidate moral depth.
  • It: Balthasar Vol. I or Gilson; Heidegger essay; write 1 curatorial essay aligning beauty/being; produce photo series as accompaniment. Why: deepen aesthetic philosophy.
  • Its: Rosenfeld/Morville & Arango; Cooper; Young; ship an IA for a real domain; run 1 longitudinal study (n ≥ 12, 6 weeks). Why: consolidate information architecture.

Year 6 — Synthesis

  • Theme A (Authority & Symbol): pair CW14 passages with constitutional design; write a 25‑page white paper; host a seminar with peers. Why: synthesise authority across quadrants.
  • Theme B (Form & Justice): pair type/space decisions with procedural fairness; create a digital artifact; conduct an ethical audit. Why: synthesise form and justice.

Year 7 — Transmission

  • Teach & Codify: teach a short course per quadrant (4 × 90 min); publish notes and templates; compile a methods handbook (typology intake, virtue drill sheets, design grids, IA checklists); release versioned edition. Why: transmit knowledge and codify methods.

Cadence (Non‑Negotiable)

To maintain momentum and integrate learning into daily life, the following rhythm is mandatory. All events below should be added to your personal calendar via the interactive calendar above.

Weekly

  • 5 focused reading blocks × 90 min (Mon–Fri)
  • 3 making blocks × 120 min (design/code/write)
  • 1 ethics drill (case brief or service hour)
  • Dream log daily; 1 analysis session per week

Monthly

  • 1 public artifact (essay, layout, prototype, or case memo)
  • 1 critique session with an external practitioner
  • 1 dialogue circle or mentoring hour

Quarterly

  • Capstone per quadrant: a shipped piece with acceptance criteria
  • Retrospective: 2 pages – what changed in your judgments, images, and interfaces

Metrics (Leading Indicators)

These dashboards help you track whether you are on pace to meet the learning objectives. Progress is aggregated from the tasks you complete in the calendar. Aim to meet or exceed each threshold.

I – Authentic Resonance

  • Dream‑to‑day motif concordance ≥ 40 % (coded themes)
  • Correct type predictions in blind assessments ≥ 70 % after Year 2
  • Counter‑transference identification latency < 24 h

We – Moral Truth

  • Case‑to‑principle mapping accuracy ≥ 80 % vs expert rubric
  • Virtue drill adherence ≥ 85 % sessions; moral “reversals” self‑reported and traced to antecedents
  • Service hours ≥ 8 per quarter with reflection logs

It – Aesthetic Truth

  • Passed peer grid/aesthetics checks with < 3 critical faults per deliverable by Year 3
  • Artifact revision cycles ≤ 2 to reach publishable quality

Its – Semantic Truth

  • Task success (first‑time users) ≥ 80 %
  • SUS ≥ 75 by second iteration
  • Model change requests per release trending down (stability ↑)

Failure Modes & Controls

To keep yourself aligned with the programme’s intentions, regularly audit against the following failure modes. Each control is built into the cadence and calendar alerts.

  • Enantiodromia (over‑correction into opposite): tally your time per quadrant weekly; if any quadrant exceeds 45 % for three weeks, force the next week to favour the neglected one.
  • Inflation via archetypal identification: work under external supervision for typological practice; document projections; apply amplification before advice.
  • Ethical performativity without interiority: conduct hidden‑curriculum audits – track when choices are driven by optics; write a conscience‑based justification.
  • Aesthetic fetishism (form over truth): embed a “communicative sufficiency” test – if usability or comprehension < 70 %, design work is rejected regardless of beauty.
  • Systems drift (clever models, bad reality): red‑team your information architecture quarterly with naïve users; accept only failures that improve instruction clarity.

Reading/Budget Gatekeeping

Always exhaust the free corpus before moving to paid texts. Only when metrics plateau for six weeks or a concrete question demands depth should you purchase or borrow the next layer.

  • I: CW6 → CW9i → MDR → CW14 selections; then von Franz or Hillman.
  • We: Aquinas I–II, then O’Donovan/Ratzinger; add Hart/Wright if metaphysical scope is required.
  • It: Bringhurst → Lupton → Balthasar or Heidegger per inquiry.
  • Its: Krug → Rosenfeld/Morville → Cooper → Covert/Young.

Proof of Learning (Annual Milestones)

These deliverables anchor your mastery at the end of each year. Use the upload features in your chosen storage to collect files and artefacts.

  • Year 1: Portfolio with 12 artifacts + 12 summaries + 6 case briefs.
  • Year 2: Function‑ethics‑form triad dossier (30–50 pages).
  • Year 3: Mechanism bundle: Model A report, applied ethics set, type booklet, UX study.
  • Year 4: Expression stack shipped (practice, policy, artifact, interface).
  • Year 5: Depth essays × 2 + advanced product IA.
  • Year 6: Two synthesis works.
  • Year 7: Methods handbook v1.0 + four recorded lectures.

Minimal Tooling

The following tools and templates support efficient execution. Use them as starting points and customise them as you grow more proficient.

  • Zettelkasten: ID system and link discipline for notes.
  • Design stack: grid templates; type tester; baseline grid document.
  • Research stack: consent form; screener; protocol; template report.
  • Ethics templates: Markkula‑style worksheet; case memo boilerplate.

Periodic Self‑Calibration Prompts

Ask yourself these questions regularly to remain balanced and to spot emerging blind spots.

  • I: “What compensates my one‑sidedness this month? What new symbol is asking for form?”
  • We: “Which virtue actually moved a decision? Which vice is rationalised as ‘strategy’?”
  • It: “What proportion decision improved comprehension? What beauty tempted falsity?”
  • Its: “Where did feedback reduce uncertainty? Which control loop is missing?”
